Bratislava

How to buy tickets?
As a city of less than half a million people, Bratislava's public transport network is somewhat compact compared to bigger cities. There is no subway network nor a network of dedicated commuter trains (though standard intercity trains have been added into the network if travelling to or from nearby towns).

The network heavily relies on trams which meet in the centre of the city and then a line goes to most of the major suburbs. This network is the one the city focuses its expansion efforts into the most as well. This is supplemented by a dense network of trolleybuses and buses all over the city. An interesting fact is that until recently the network even included buses to both Austria and Hungary as this Slovak capital lies on the border with both of them - the lines have, however, been cancelled due to low profit.
Trams

Trams are this city's bearing mode of transport - especially if necessary to get through a longer distance. The city is now focusing on expanding the network to the south side of the river as currently a vast majority is in the north.

Trolleybuses

Trolleybuses are another part of the network of public transport that the city very heavily relies on - there are trolleybus lines close to anywhere within the city on all the important lines that are in use. Currently, there are expansion plans to the airport.

Buses

As in any other city, buses are present in Bratislava in those spots, where infrastructure for trams or trolleybuses has not been set up - these may typically be less crucial lines. The bus network is very dense and reaching into the neighboring towns as well.
