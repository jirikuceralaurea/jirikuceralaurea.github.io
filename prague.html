Prague

How to buy tickets?
This central European capital boasts a dense public transport network. The bearing mode of transport in this case is the metro which currently consists of 3 lines marked by letters. The metro will quickly get you from one side of the city to the other. This is supplemented by a dense network of trams - one of the densest in Europe.

Within the wider city centre, these modes of transport will get you anywhere. Outside of the city centre, you may also want to use buses. There is only one trolleybus line in the whole city so far, but two fun methods of transport that you can use are tiny ferries from one side of the river to the other and a funicular to the Petřín hill - both included in any ticket for at least 24 hours.
Metro

The metro has 3 lines that cross in the city centre where you can switch. It has been operating since the 1970s and is an efficient way to get around the city quick and easy - it's also simple to use for tourists.

Trams

The tram network is large and can get you just about anywhere in the city - it has also been recently expanding so even some of the suburbs and even nearby towns are now served by trams.

Funicular

This is rather a fun tip - you can use the funicular up to the Petřín hill to have a little walk around the park and take in the view. It runs all year except a few weeks in October.

Buses

Buses complement the other modes of transport - they are mostly present in the densely populated suburb areas. They also run at night with night trams when the metro is off.
