Welcome to Public transport around the world!

Public transportation is one of the things that is quite tricky to get a grasp of when visiting a new city or relocating. Therefore, on this page I would like to start a collection of the most important information about public transportation in different cities, to give you a hint of how the public transportation works wherever you are planning to visit.

The cities currently available on this website are Prague and Bratislava, central European capitals with fairly large networks of public transportation to offer to their citizens.

You will be able to see which methods of transport these cities include - this also hints at how advanced the public transport network in a given city is and how fast it allows the citizens of the given city to move.
Prague

Prague is the capital city of the Czech Republic - its very affordable network of public transport is lead by the city's metro lines and supplemented by one of the highest density tram networks in Europe.

Bratislava

Bratislava is the capital city of Slovakia - its network does not contain metro, but for a city its size, the ever improving tram network supplemented by trolleybuses does the job.

Berlin

The German capital contains a dense network of a subway, commuter trains and trams. Its efficient system reaches all around the cities and has quite a few interesting pecularities.
